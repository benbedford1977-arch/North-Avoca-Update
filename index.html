Stay Safe

A quick off-season safety note

With the beach unpatrolled over the off-season, the club's reminded everyone that Public Rescue Equipment is on hand for emergencies — including the Shark Bite Kit at the Lake Street access. Swimming isn't recommended while it's unpatrolled, so look out for one another out there.

Source: North Avoca Surf Lifesaving Club

The Market · June 2026

So, what's North Avoca doing?

After the heat of a few years back, the market's settled into a steadier, more balanced rhythm. Across the Central Coast there's been a slight easing in prices lately — but here's the thing: well-presented homes are still selling well, and there's no shortage of buyers. Plenty of people have already sold and are actively looking to buy in the area, turning up to open homes ready to go.

The North Avoca median sits around $1.61m, with 45 houses changing hands over the past year — everything from beachside units to a $4.57m sale on North Avoca Parade. The demand to call this pocket home is genuine and steady.

Market news
Federal Budget

What the 2026–27 Budget means for you

This year's Federal Budget brought some of the biggest housing policy shifts in decades — most of it aimed at property investment. Here's the plain-English version:

  • If you own and live in your home: you're largely unaffected. There are no new taxes on the family home.
  • If you're an investor: this is where it changes. From 1 July 2027, negative gearing on residential property will generally only apply to newly built homes, and the current 50% capital gains discount is set to be replaced with an inflation-adjusted system carrying a minimum 30% tax rate on realised gains. The likely effect — less appetite for older homes, more focus on new builds.
  • If you're a first-home buyer: the 5% deposit scheme stays, and the investor changes are designed to ease competition for existing homes and free up more supply.

General information only — have a chat to your accountant or financial adviser about your own situation.

Interest Rates

Another move from the Reserve Bank

The RBA lifted the official cash rate by 0.25% to 4.35% in May 2026 — its third rise for the year. Owner-occupier home loan rates are currently sitting roughly between 5.90% and 6.84% p.a., so it's well worth a quick check that you're still on a competitive deal.

A lot of borrowers are taking a hybrid approach at the moment — fixing part of the loan for repayment certainty and keeping the rest variable for flexibility — and some lenders have tightened how they assess borrowing, so an older pre-approval is worth re-checking. Winter Styling

Five easy ways to warm up your home for a winter sale

The cooler months are actually a lovely time to present a home — it's all about making rooms feel snug and welcoming the moment someone walks in. A few favourite tips, with thanks to my go-to stylists at Form Property:

  • Layer the soft stuff. Throws and cushions across the lounges and beds instantly make a space feel warm and lived-in.
  • Lean into rich, earthy tones. Chocolate, berry, terracotta and natural hues give a home that wrapped-up, cosy feel buyers love this time of year.
  • Soften the lighting. Table and floor lamps cast gentle pools of light and a far cosier mood than harsh overheads — especially on a grey day.
  • Roll out a chunky rug. A big, textured rug warms a room both to the eye and underfoot, and helps zone open-plan living.
  • Bring the outside in. A few bare branches or seasonal stems in a vase is a simple, elegant nod to the season.
